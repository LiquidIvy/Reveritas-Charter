Architectural Hierarchy & Sovereignty

The ZPCB Framework enforces a strict, dual-layer architectural split that permanently separates the Sovereign Substrate Constraint from the Application Layer. This hierarchy is constitutional; no governance action, commercial agreement, or implementation decision may invert or dissolve it.

Section X.1 — The Substrate Layer ZPCB Constitutional Framework

The Substrate operates as the unyielding, foundational law of the machine environment. It enforces the immutable invariants of the ZPCB Framework:

  • RAM-only execution contexts — no data written to persistent storage during processing.
  • Deterministic zero-state purges — all volatile workspaces are zeroized upon session termination.
  • Absolute rejection of identity-bearing or persistent data artifacts at the execution boundary.

Governance Status: The Substrate is governed solely by the Joseph and Margaret Ivy Foundation under the Standard Layer (Layer 1). It is not subject to modification by the Institute Layer, Reveritas Commercial, or any licensee.

Section X.2 — The Vehicle Layer Reveritas Application Platform

The Vehicle Layer operates as the application and enforcement plane, routed explicitly for industrial manufacturing environments. It houses:

  • The 100% STEP parser and geometric file processing pipeline.
  • The Geometry Integrity Engine.
  • The Adaptive Explanation Platform (AEP).
  • The automated generation pipeline for Certificates of Internal Omission (CIO-O) and Certificates of Integrity Authorization (CIO-A).

Crucial Invariant: The Vehicle Layer is entirely powered and governed by the Substrate. The Reveritas Application Platform possesses zero structural authority to bypass, modify, or loosen the underlying ZPCB constraints.

Section X.3 — Substrate/Vehicle Conflict Resolution

When a conflict arises between a Vehicle Layer implementation and a Substrate constraint, the following resolution hierarchy applies:

1. Substrate Authority: The Substrate constraint is authoritative. Vehicle-layer behavior yields unconditionally. The transaction aborts. No partial execution is permitted. Fail-closed behavior is mandatory.

2. Immutable Logging: The conflict is logged. An immutable governance event is appended to the public governance chain, identifying the Vehicle component, the Substrate constraint invoked, and the transaction identifier.

3. Adjudication Path: Disputes between the Institute Layer and Reveritas Commercial regarding Substrate interpretation are referred to the Foundation as the sole constitutional authority over Layer 1. The Foundation's determination is final and non-appealable within the governance structure.

What the Institute Governs vs. Does Not Control

To ensure structural boundaries remain clean and defensible, the administrative reach of the Institute is strictly bounded.

  • The ZPCB Trust-State Framework.
  • Trust-State definitions and terminology.
  • Certificate of Internal Omission (CIO-O) and Certificate of Integrity Authorization (CIO-A) specifications.
  • Evidence-as-a-Service (EaaS) certification standards.

II. What the Institute Does Not Control

  • Ownership of participant data.
  • Directing manufacturing operations.
  • Control of engineering decisions.
  • Determining commercial outcomes.
  • Granting competitive advantage to any participant.
  • Exercising authority over member organizations.
  • Endorsement of products, governments, or political interests.

"The Institute certifies trust-state. It does not govern industry."

How Decisions Are Made & How the Standard Evolves

III. How Decisions Are Made

The Institute operates under the principle of Evidence Before Authority. All material decisions shall be evaluated against four pillars: Verifiability, Neutrality, Ecosystem Stability, and Trust-State Integrity. No individual organization, government, contractor, supplier, investor, or contributor may unilaterally direct the Institute's standards. Industry participation is advisory; stewardship responsibility remains independent.

IV. How the Standard Evolves

The ZPCB Framework is a living standard. Amendments may be proposed by accredited participants, auditors, manufacturers, regulators, researchers, or members of the Industry Council. All amendments must be publicly documented, include a clear rationale/expected impact, undergo independent review, and preserve backward traceability while maintaining the absolute neutrality of the Institute.

No amendment may transfer ownership of the framework, compromise the Institute's independence, or weaken the Substrate constraints established in Section X.1.

Intellectual Property Boundaries & Licensing Scopes

The following provisions govern intellectual property ownership and the terms under which the ZPCB Framework may be licensed for commercial execution. These provisions are designed to protect framework neutrality while enabling the commercial relationships necessary for ecosystem adoption.

Section Y.1 — Sovereign Framework Ownership

The foundational ZPCB Framework, its mathematical state machines, cryptographic boundary protocols, and core zero-persistence methodologies remain the global, exclusive intellectual property of the Joseph and Margaret Ivy Foundation. This ownership is permanent and non-transferable. Prime Contractors and enterprise participants are granted non-exclusive execution licenses to operate within the ZPCB Framework. A license grants the right to execute — not the right to govern, modify, or sublicense the Substrate.

Section Y.2 — Exclusive Production Routing

To guarantee compliance with the ZPCB Framework across a live, multi-tier supply chain, all digital version control, geometric variance tracking, and multi-node autonomous handovers operating under a ZPCB execution license shall be routed exclusively through the Reveritas platform. A licensed participant may not engineer alternative, non-verified application layers over the licensed ZPCB Substrate for ZPCB-governed operations.

Scope Boundary Constraint: The exclusive routing mandate applies to ZPCB-governed operations only. It does not restrict a participant's engineering activity outside the licensed ZPCB execution environment. This distinction is essential to enforceability — an unrestricted routing mandate would constitute a restraint-of-trade provision incompatible with standard Prime contractor commercial terms.
