Reveritas Institute Charter

Version 0.1

Preamble

The Reveritas Institute exists to preserve and advance trust-state integrity across regulated engineering ecosystems.

The Institute does not exist to own, operate, or control the industries it serves. Its role is to establish neutral standards, issue verifiable trust-state certifications, and maintain the integrity of the Zero-Point-Cryptographic-Boundary (ZPCB) Framework.

The Institute recognizes that trust cannot be commanded by authority alone. Trust must be earned through transparency, evidence, and independent verification.

The Reveritas Invariants

The following principles are permanent.
No amendment, council vote, commercial agreement, or governance action may violate them.

  1. Trust shall be established through evidence.
  2. Verification shall not require surrendering sovereignty.
  3. No participant may purchase control of reality.
  4. Standards remain independent of commercial influence.
  5. Every certification must be independently verifiable.
  6. Framework governance shall remain separate from implementation.
  7. The Institute exists to preserve trust-state neutrality.

I. What the Institute Governs

The Institute governs:

  • The ZPCB Trust-State Framework.
  • Trust-State definitions and terminology.
  • Certificate of Internal Omission (CIO) specifications.
  • Evidence-as-a-Service (EaaS) certification standards.
  • Trust-State verification protocols.
  • Governance requirements for accredited implementations.
  • Public trust-state registries and certification procedures.

The Institute serves as steward of these standards for the benefit of the broader ecosystem.

II. What the Institute Does Not Control

The Institute does not:

  • Own participant data.
  • Direct manufacturing operations.
  • Control engineering decisions.
  • Determine commercial outcomes.
  • Grant a competitive advantage to any participant.
  • Exercise authority over member organizations.
  • Endorse products, governments, corporations, or political interests.

The Institute certifies trust-state. It does not govern industry.

III. How Decisions Are Made

The Institute operates under the principle of Evidence Before Authority.

All material decisions shall be evaluated against:

  1. Verifiability
  2. Neutrality
  3. Ecosystem Stability
  4. Trust-State Integrity

No individual organization, government, contractor, supplier, investor, or contributor may unilaterally direct the Institute’s standards.

Industry participation is advisory.

Stewardship responsibility remains independent.

IV. How the Standard Evolves

The ZPCB Framework is a living standard.

Amendments may be proposed by accredited participants, auditors, manufacturers, regulators, researchers, or members of the Industry Council.

All amendments must:

  • Be publicly documented.
  • Include a rationale and expected impact.
  • Undergo independent review.
  • Preserve backward traceability.
  • Maintain the neutrality of the Institute.

No amendment may transfer ownership of the framework or compromise the Institute’s independence.

V. Governance Architecture

Layer 1 — The Standard Layer

The ZPCB Standard, Trust-State definitions, CIO specifications, and Boundary Cycle protocols live here. This layer is governed by supermajority amendment only, published openly, and versioned with the same hash-chain integrity as a CIO. No single member, no single contributor, no founder can unilaterally modify it. Changes require demonstrated consensus across all three ecosystem circles and a mandatory public comment period. Critically — the inevitable Joseph and Margaret Ivy Foundation is the home for this layer. It holds the IP and the constitutional documents.

Layer 2 — The Institute Layer

This is the operational body that issues, revokes, and adjudicates CIOs. Governed by an independent board whose members are explicitly prohibited from commercial relationships with Reveritas Commercial or any Industry Council member. Term-limited. Published conflict-of-interest disclosures. Decisions are logged to a public governance chain — the same architecture as the CIO's hash chain, but applied to institutional governance events. The key design constraint: the Institute cannot be funded by volume. Membership dues and certification fees must be pooled and allocated by formula, not by relationship — otherwise fee dependency recreates the capture problem.

Layer 3 — The Commercial Layer

Reveritas Commercial operates as a separate entity, licensed by the Institute to provide EaaS, verification infrastructure, and enterprise services. The separation is load-bearing: the Institute certifies; Reveritas Commercial enables. When an organization gets a CIO, it's not Reveritas Commercial that makes it authoritative — it's the Institute. Reveritas Commercial is the railroad. The Standard is the law of the land.

Foundational Principle

Trust shall not be granted through status.

Trust shall be established through evidence.

The purpose of the Reveritas Institute is not to become the center of the ecosystem, but to ensure that every participant can verify reality without surrendering sovereignty.

Verify Everything. Retain Nothing.
