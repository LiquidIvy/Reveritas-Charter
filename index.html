Reveritas Institute Charter [cite: 1]

Version 0.1 [cite: 1]

Preamble [cite: 1]

The Reveritas Institute exists to preserve and advance trust-state integrity across regulated engineering ecosystems. [cite: 1]

The Institute does not exist to own, operate, or control the industries it serves. [cite: 1] Its role is to establish neutral standards, issue verifiable trust-state certifications, and maintain the integrity of the Zero-Phase-Clock-Buffer (ZPCB) Framework. [cite: 1]

The Institute recognizes that trust cannot be commanded by authority alone. [cite: 1] Trust must be earned through transparency, evidence, and independent verification. [cite: 1]

I. What the Institute Governs [cite: 1]

The Institute governs: [cite: 1]

  • The ZPCB Trust-State Framework. [cite: 1]
  • Trust-State definitions and terminology. [cite: 1]
  • Certificate of Internal Omission (CIO) specifications. [cite: 1]
  • Evidence-as-a-Service (EaaS) certification standards. [cite: 1]
  • Trust-State verification protocols. [cite: 1]
  • Governance requirements for accredited implementations. [cite: 1]
  • Public trust-state registries and certification procedures. [cite: 1]

The Institute serves as steward of these standards for the benefit of the broader ecosystem. [cite: 1]

II. What the Institute Does Not Control [cite: 1]

The Institute does not: [cite: 1]

  • Own participant data. [cite: 1]
  • Direct manufacturing operations. [cite: 1]
  • Control engineering decisions. [cite: 1]
  • Determine commercial outcomes. [cite: 1]
  • Grant a competitive advantage to any participant. [cite: 1]
  • Exercise authority over member organizations. [cite: 1]
  • Endorse products, governments, corporations, or political interests. [cite: 1]

The Institute certifies trust-state. [cite: 1] It does not govern industry. [cite: 1]

III. How Decisions Are Made [cite: 1]

The Institute operates under the principle of Evidence Before Authority. [cite: 1]

All material decisions shall be evaluated against: [cite: 1]

  1. Verifiability [cite: 1]
  2. Neutrality [cite: 1]
  3. Ecosystem Stability [cite: 1]
  4. Trust-State Integrity [cite: 1]

No individual organization, government, contractor, supplier, investor, or contributor may unilaterally direct the Institute’s standards. [cite: 1]

Industry participation is advisory. [cite: 1]

Stewardship responsibility remains independent. [cite: 1]

IV. How the Standard Evolves [cite: 1]

The ZPCB Framework is a living standard. [cite: 1]

Amendments may be proposed by accredited participants, auditors, manufacturers, regulators, researchers, or members of the Industry Council. [cite: 1]

All amendments must: [cite: 1]

  • Be publicly documented. [cite: 1]
  • Include a rationale and expected impact. [cite: 1]
  • Undergo independent review. [cite: 1]
  • Preserve backward traceability. [cite: 1]
  • Maintain the neutrality of the Institute. [cite: 1]

No amendment may transfer ownership of the framework or compromise the Institute’s independence. [cite: 1]

Foundational Principle [cite: 1]

Trust shall not be granted through status. [cite: 1]

Trust shall be established through evidence. [cite: 1]

The purpose of the Reveritas Institute is not to become the center of the ecosystem, but to ensure that every participant can verify reality without surrendering sovereignty. [cite: 1]

Verify Everything. [cite: 1] Retain Nothing. [cite: 1]
